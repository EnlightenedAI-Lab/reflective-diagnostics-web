Who it’s for

This pilot is designed for teams using LLMs in real workflows and looking for stronger evidence before scaling usage, choosing models, or expanding deployment.

Enterprise AI teams

Organizations deploying internal copilots, knowledge assistants, or AI-supported workflows where answer quality matters.

Product and AI leaders

Teams comparing models or system variants and needing more than surface-level demos or benchmark scores.

Risk-sensitive use cases

Environments where fluent but miscalibrated answers can create downstream cost, confusion, or loss of trust.

What the pilot measures

The evaluation focuses on whether a system is right, how it expresses certainty, and where its behavior becomes unreliable under structured testing.

Correctness

Tests whether outputs are actually correct on defined task sets, not merely plausible or well-worded.

Confidence alignment

Examines whether the model’s expressed confidence remains aligned with verified truth rather than overstating certainty.

Failure patterns

Surfaces where the system overclaims, hedges, contradicts itself, or fails in ways that matter for deployment decisions.

A model can sound fluent and persuasive while still being wrong. The deeper question is whether it remains reliable enough to trust in the workflows that matter.

What the client receives

A fixed-scope engagement resulting in a substantive written evaluation report, often in the range of 10–15 pages depending on scope, designed to support real deployment, benchmarking, and model-selection decisions.

Core deliverables

  • A structured written evaluation report, typically 10–15 pages depending on scope
  • Assessment of the defined task set or workflow across relevant reliability metrics
  • Analysis of correctness, confidence alignment, and answer quality
  • Comparison across selected models or system variants
  • Identification of high-risk failure patterns and points of breakdown
  • Written interpretation of key findings and their practical implications
  • Live walkthrough of results and recommended next steps

Why this has practical value

  • Provides a concrete basis for deciding which model is more dependable for a given workflow
  • Helps determine whether a system is ready for broader deployment
  • Shows where current behavior creates unnecessary operational or reputational risk
  • Surfaces failure modes that may not appear in ordinary testing or demos
  • Gives teams a more defensible basis for internal review, prioritization, and improvement

How the pilot works

The engagement is structured as a focused evaluation rather than an open-ended consulting project.

Step 1

Define scope

Select the workflow, task set, or model comparison objective.

Step 2

Run evaluation

Apply structured testing to assess correctness, confidence alignment, and failure patterns.

Step 3

Review findings

Summarize key results, notable failure modes, and practical implications.

Step 4

Identify next steps

Discuss whether deeper evaluation or broader deployment review is warranted.
