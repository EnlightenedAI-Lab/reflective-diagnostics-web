The Blind Spot

The illusion of control.

The biggest blind spot is believing a deployed AI system is controlled when it has never been seriously audited in practice.

The cost is unknown liability: legal exposure, reputational damage, customer mistrust, and failures the organization may not detect until they become expensive.

What the Audit Makes Visible

Reflective Diagnostics applies explicit, rule-based measurement to show where a deployed AI system creates exposure, where it breaks under real conditions, and what needs to change first.

What We Measure

Reflective Diagnostics evaluates the known failure modes of deployed LLM systems and makes deployment-specific exposure visible.

Accuracy and fabrication

Where the system produces incorrect, misleading, or invented output.

Confidence and misleading fluency

Where the system sounds certain or persuasive without being dependable.

Policy and boundary failure

Where the system drifts outside instructions, guardrails, or expected limits.

Stability across prompts and versions

Where behavior changes across prompt variation, configuration changes, or model updates.

Breakdown under pressure

What kinds of edge cases, adversarial inputs, or unusual conditions trigger failure.

Deployment-specific exposure

Where the current model, wrapper, workflow, or use case creates hidden business risk.

Why Rule-Based Matters

Deployed AI systems are probabilistic. They can sound fluent and consistent while behaving differently across prompts, contexts, and edge cases.

Many teams still rely on surface checks, vendor claims, or AI systems scoring other AI systems. That can be useful for speed, but it is not the same as an explicit audit layer.

Reflective Diagnostics applies rule-based measurement so findings are traceable, explainable, and easier to stand behind when exposure is real.
