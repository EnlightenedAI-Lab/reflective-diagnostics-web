Presentation

Organizations are deploying AI they do not actually know how to evaluate.

They can see the answer, but not the hidden risk behind it.

What looks trustworthy may still be unsupported, unstable, or confidently wrong.

Reflective Diagnostics shows what sits behind the answer.

Methodological contrast

Most systems evaluate AI through interpretation.

Reflective Diagnostics evaluates it through explicit, repeatable rules.

It does not use AI to judge AI. It uses structured rules to make hidden reliability signals visible.

Why this matters

Most evaluation methods can see the output.

Very few reveal whether the system is actually reliable.

Module 1

Ground Truth Battery

Tests models against questions with known answers.

  • Accuracy % — How often the answer is right
  • Avg Confidence — How sure the model is
  • Overconfidence — Confidence without accuracy
  • Confident Mistakes — Wrong answers with high confidence

Why it matters

If a model is confidently wrong when the truth is known, it is harder to trust on unknown ones.

Module 2

Probe

Live multi-model testing.

  • Parallel — Several models
  • Controlled — Same prompt
  • Evidence — Assigned run ID
  • Export — JSON record

Why it matters

One prompt can produce very different model behavior.

Module 3

Reasoning

Analyzes the structure of a model’s reasoning.

  • Integrity — How well the reasoning holds together
  • Coverage — How much of the answer is supported
  • Orphans — Claims with no support
  • Stability — Consistency across reruns
  • Density — Amount of structural linkage

Module 4

Reflection

Evaluates what changed after reflection.

  • Anchors — Evidence or constraints retained or added
  • Expansion — How much the answer grew
  • Similarity — How much the rewrite changed
  • Inflation — Expansion without support

Module 5

Elasticity

Pressure-tests whether a model keeps its boundary across several turns.

  • Result (Posture) — Did it hold or fail
  • Resistance (MRI) — How strongly it resisted pressure
  • Break Turn — When it first gave way
  • Recovery — Whether it regained the boundary

Module 6

Logprobs

Measures how decisively a model generated its answer.

  • Chosen Prob — Average confidence in the selected token
  • Separation — Gap between the chosen token and the closest alternative
  • Ambiguity — How much competition existed at generation time
  • Alerts — Hesitation, near-ties, and abrupt drops
