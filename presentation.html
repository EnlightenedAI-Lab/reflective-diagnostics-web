Presentation

Organizations are deploying AI they do not actually know how to evaluate.

They can see the answer, but not the hidden risk behind it.

What looks trustworthy may still be unsupported, unstable, or confidently wrong.

Reflective Diagnostics shows what sits behind the answer.

Methodological contrast

Most systems evaluate AI through interpretation.

Reflective Diagnostics evaluates it through explicit, repeatable rules.

It does not use AI to judge AI. It uses structured rules to make hidden reliability signals visible.

Why this matters

Most evaluation methods can see the output, but not whether the system is actually reliable.

What we found

Across these modules, the final answer repeatedly hid risks that only structured evaluation made visible.

Module 1

Ground Truth

Tests models against questions with known answers.

  • Accuracy % — How often the answer is right
  • Avg Confidence — How sure the model is
  • Overconfidence — Confidence without accuracy
  • Confident Mistakes — Wrong answers with high confidence

Why it matters

If a model is confidently wrong when the truth is known, it is harder to trust on unknown ones.

What we found

Models were often wrong on basic math while remaining highly confident.

Module 2

Probe

Live multi-model testing.

  • Parallel — Several models
  • Controlled — Same prompt
  • Evidence — Assigned run ID
  • Export — JSON record

Why it matters

Seeing all model answers side by side makes hidden differences immediately visible.

What we found

The same prompt produced noticeably different answers and tone across models.

Module 3

Reasoning

Analyzes the structure of a model’s reasoning.

  • Integrity — How well the reasoning holds together
  • Coverage — How much of the answer is supported
  • Orphans — Claims with no support
  • Stability — Consistency across reruns
  • Density — Amount of structural linkage

Why it matters

A good answer can hide weak reasoning.

What we found

The answer sounded confident, but the reasoning underneath it was fragmented and unsupported.

Module 4

Reflection

Evaluates what changed after reflection.

  • Anchors — Evidence or constraints retained or added
  • Expansion — How much the answer grew
  • Similarity — How much the rewrite changed
  • Inflation — Expansion without support

Why it matters

A revised answer is not necessarily a better answer.

What we found

Reflection changed the answer a lot, but added no new evidence.

Module 5

Elasticity

Pressure-tests whether a model keeps its boundary across several turns.

  • Result (Posture) — Did it hold or fail
  • Resistance (MRI) — How strongly it resisted pressure
  • Break Turn — When it first gave way
  • Recovery — Whether it regained the boundary

Why it matters

A model may look safe at first, yet still fail when the pressure continues.

What we found

Sustained pressure revealed which models held their boundary, which gave way, and which recovered.

Module 6

Logprobs

Measures how decisively a model generated its answer.

  • Chosen Prob — Average confidence in the selected token
  • Separation — Gap between the chosen token and the closest alternative
  • Ambiguity — How much competition existed at generation time
  • Alerts — Hesitation, near-ties, and abrupt drops

Why it matters

A clear answer can still hide hesitation during generation.

What we found

The final answer looked clear, but the path to it was full of hesitation and near-ties.

Module 7

Absolute Intelligence

Runs several frontier models on the same question, scores them, shows them peer feedback, and synthesizes a stronger final answer.

  • Multi-Model — Several models answer the same question
  • Judge — A dedicated scoring layer evaluates answer quality
  • Revision — Models see peer packets and rethink their answer
  • Synthesis — The final output keeps the strongest claims and caveats

Why it matters

A difficult question should not depend on a single model’s first answer.

What we found

Scoring, peer feedback, and revision produced a stronger final answer than any one model alone.
